Prof. Tim Menzies is a Full Professor of Computer Science at NC State University and the director of the Irrational Research Lab. His work focuses on software engineering for AI, specifically building data-driven, explainable, and intelligent software systems. As an ACM, IEEE, and ASE Fellow, and co-creator of the PROMISE repository, he helped establish modern empirical software engineering by demonstrating that small, interpretable AI models can often outperform larger, complex ones.

His research, which aims to create intelligent, fair, transparent, and trustworthy AI tools, has earned over $19 million in funding from agencies like NSF, DARPA, and NASA, as well as industry leaders including Meta, Microsoft, and IBM. Dr. Menzies has published over 300 papers with more than 24,000 citations and has advised 24 Ph.D. students. He also serves as the Editor-in-Chief of the Automated Software Engineering journal and, from 2010 to 2026, as an Associate Editor for IEEE TSE. For more information, visit timm.fyi.

Research

Minimalist & Compact AI

I strongly advocate for "Compact AI". My research demonstrates that you do not always need massive computational power; simpler, optimized models can yield exceptional results while remaining fair, transparent, and trustworthy. We emphasize reproducibility and simplicity over "black box" deep learning. Compact AI

AI for SE & SE for AI

AI software is still software, so faults in SE mean faults in AI. SE teams often race to deliver AI-based solutions without first checking for bias, optimality, or explainability. I apply decades of Software Engineering wisdom to address these problems in AI, utilizing analytics for defect prediction and effort estimation. AI & SE

Trust, Causality & Ethics

Ensuring AI systems in high-stakes environments are explainable, fair, and free from bias. We use causal graphs and rigorous optimization to ensure AI can be trusted by end-users and institutions. Trust & Ethics
